About

James Hupp is a person. He leads content strategy at the U.S. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. At present, he's mostly working on a site refresh. (The team would love you to use, share, or critique the code!)

James's career began in campaigns. Once upon a time, he was an email strategist for progressive non-profits. In 2008, he joined Obama for America to help grassroots supporters raise money on their own. And in 2009, he joined a new progressive digital strategy firm.

In 2010, a campaign colleague asked James to join a startup government agency. He came on as the lead writer for the new CFPB's even newer website. Campaigning had taught him to motivate an audience; government quickly taught him that the ongoing churn of information had to be sustainable for both audience and staff. The light switch flipped. He's been a content strategist since.

James is originally from Kansas City, Mo. Today, he spends a lot of time in both Washington and Brooklyn. He watches a lot of soccer and listens to a lot of music. His nascent travel habit is abetted by his delightful girlfriend; his pickling habit is abetted, sadly, by nobody. He believes in being nice.
