James Hupp is a person. He lives, for the most part, in Washington, D.C., but spends about 20 percent of his life in New York. He is the content strategy lead at the U.S. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. The CFPB, as it's also known, is helps consumer finance markets work by making consumer finance rules more effective, consistently and fairly enforcing those rules, and empowering consumers to take more control over their economic lives. At present, most of his work is wrapped up in a full refresh of the website. All of us encourage you to use and share the code associated with the new designs.

James's work history is far more political than his work present. He was the Vice President of a progressive digital strategy firm. In 2008, he managed the digital grassroots fundraising tools for Obama for America, after which he wrote for the 2009 Presidential Inauguration. And once, years ago, he was an email campaigner for progressive non-profits.

James is from Kansas City (Missouri, to answer the most common follow-up question). His youth taught him to love barbecue. It also taught him that sports fans are doomed to cruelty, hopelessness, and pain.
